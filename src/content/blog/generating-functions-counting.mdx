## Introduction

A **generating function** encodes a sequence $(a_n)$ as a formal power series:

$$
A(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n x^n
$$

This transforms combinatorial problems into algebraic manipulations.

## Examples

**Geometric series:** $a_n = 1$ gives $A(x) = \frac{1}{1-x}$.

**Fibonacci:** The generating function satisfies $F(x) = x + xF(x) + x^2F(x)$:

$$
F(x) = \frac{x}{1 - x - x^2}
$$

Partial fractions give Binet's formula: $F_n = \frac{\varphi^n - \hat{\varphi}^n}{\sqrt{5}}$.

**Catalan numbers:** $C_n = \frac{1}{n+1}\binom{2n}{n}$ has generating function:

$$
C(x) = \frac{1 - \sqrt{1-4x}}{2x}
$$

satisfying $C(x) = 1 + xC(x)^2$.

## Operations and Their Meanings

| Operation on $A(x)$ | Effect on $(a_n)$ |
|---|---|
| $xA(x)$ | Shift: $a_{n-1}$ |
| $A'(x)$ | $(n+1)a_{n+1}$ |
| $A(x)B(x)$ | Convolution: $\sum_{k} a_k b_{n-k}$ |
| $\frac{1}{1-x}A(x)$ | Partial sums: $\sum_{k \leq n} a_k$ |

## Exponential Generating Functions

For labeled structures, use $\hat{A}(x) = \sum a_n \frac{x^n}{n!}$. Then products correspond to combining labeled structures:

$$
D_n = n! \sum_{k=0}^{n} \frac{(-1)^k}{k!} \quad \text{(derangements, with EGF } \frac{e^{-x}}{1-x}\text{)}
$$

## Key Takeaways

1. Generating functions turn recursions into algebraic equations.
2. Products of OGFs = convolutions = counting compositions.
3. The "transfer principle" connects combinatorial operations to algebraic operations.
