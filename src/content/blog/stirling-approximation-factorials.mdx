## Introduction

For large $n$, the factorial grows super-exponentially. **Stirling's approximation** tames it:

$$
n! \sim \sqrt{2\pi n} \left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n
$$

meaning the ratio approaches $1$ as $n \to \infty$. The relative error is $\sim \frac{1}{12n}$.

## Proof Sketch via the Gamma Function

Since $n! = \Gamma(n+1) = \int_0^\infty t^n e^{-t}\,dt$, substitute $t = n + \sqrt{n}\,u$:

$$
n! = n^{n+1/2}e^{-n} \int_{-\sqrt{n}}^{\infty} \left(1 + \frac{u}{\sqrt{n}}\right)^n e^{-\sqrt{n}u}\,du
$$

As $n \to \infty$, the integrand converges to $e^{-u^2/2}$, giving:

$$
n! \sim n^{n+1/2}e^{-n} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-u^2/2}\,du = \sqrt{2\pi n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n
$$

## Refinement

The full asymptotic series:

$$
n! = \sqrt{2\pi n}\left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n \left(1 + \frac{1}{12n} + \frac{1}{288n^2} - \frac{139}{51840n^3} + \cdots\right)
$$

## Applications

**Binomial coefficient:** $\binom{2n}{n} \sim \frac{4^n}{\sqrt{\pi n}}$

**Entropy:** $\log(n!) \approx n\log n - n + \frac{1}{2}\log(2\pi n)$

**Central Limit Theorem:** Stirling's approximation is key to proving the CLT for sums of i.i.d. random variables.

## Key Takeaways

1. Stirling's approximation $n! \sim \sqrt{2\pi n}(n/e)^n$ is accurate to within $1/(12n)$ relative error.
2. The proof uses the Laplace method (saddle-point approximation) of the Gamma integral.
3. It's essential in combinatorics, statistical mechanics, and probability theory.
