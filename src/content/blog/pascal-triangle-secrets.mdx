## Introduction

**Pascal's triangle** arranges the binomial coefficients $\binom{n}{k}$ in a triangle where each entry is the sum of the two entries above it:

$$
\binom{n}{k} = \binom{n-1}{k-1} + \binom{n-1}{k}
$$

## Hidden Patterns

**Row sums:** $\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} = 2^n$ (count subsets of an $n$-set).

**Alternating sums:** $\sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^k \binom{n}{k} = 0$ for $n \geq 1$.

**Diagonal sums give Fibonacci:** $\sum_{k} \binom{n-k}{k} = F_{n+1}$.

**Hockey stick identity:**

$$
\sum_{i=r}^{n} \binom{i}{r} = \binom{n+1}{r+1}
$$

## Vandermonde's Identity

$$
\binom{m+n}{r} = \sum_{k=0}^{r} \binom{m}{k}\binom{n}{r-k}
$$

This is the coefficient of $x^r$ in $(1+x)^m(1+x)^n = (1+x)^{m+n}$.

## Pascal's Triangle mod $p$

Color entries by divisibility by prime $p$. The result is the **Sierpiński triangle** — a fractal! This is explained by **Lucas's theorem**:

$$
\binom{n}{k} \equiv \prod_{i} \binom{n_i}{k_i} \pmod{p}
$$

where $n = \sum n_i p^i$ and $k = \sum k_i p^i$ are base-$p$ representations.

## Key Takeaways

1. Pascal's triangle encodes binomial coefficients and combinatorial identities.
2. The diagonal sums give Fibonacci numbers — connecting two fundamental sequences.
3. Modular reduction reveals fractal structure (Sierpiński triangle).
